Portfolio

The NUCLEO Portfolio

Four vertically integrated operations across the Yucatan Peninsula. Each runs independently and contributes to a shared infrastructure layer. Together, they form a self-reinforcing network that compounds revenue, reduces operating costs, and builds institutional value over time.

The Model

Built to compound, not compete

Each NUCLEO operation is independently viable with its own revenue model, guest base, and operating team. But their real value comes from integration. A student certified by Scorpio Divers joins a Yucatan Dive Trek expedition. A guest of Casa Nahal books through XTC. Every venture feeds every other.

Shared logistics, staff, marketing, and safety infrastructure reduce operating costs across all four sites. The result: each venture is more resilient and more profitable than it could be operating in isolation.

Why NUCLEO Works

How the network compounds

Three structural advantages make the NUCLEO portfolio more resilient and more profitable than its individual parts.

01

Shared Infrastructure

One fleet, one operations team, one booking system, one HR payroll, and one safety compliance framework serve all four ventures. Fixed costs that would otherwise sit on a single P&L are distributed across the network. Each operation benefits from institutional-grade infrastructure at a fraction of the standalone cost.

Cost Structure
02

Commercial Compounding

Yucatan Dive Trek owns the inbound distribution channel for all four venues. Scorpio Divers certifies divers who then book XTC expeditions. XTC guests stay at The Dark Zone. Guests join multi-day expeditions. Each client touchpoint generates revenue across multiple ventures without additional acquisition cost.

Revenue Model
03

Unified Data Stack

The PROTEUS stack feeds a real-time data lake that aggregates unit economics, logistics, and client data across all four operations. Decisions on staffing, pricing, inventory, and capacity are made with full network visibility. Owners and investors receive a single reporting package that reflects the portfolio as a whole, not four separate P&Ls.
