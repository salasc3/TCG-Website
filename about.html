Our Story

Operators. Builders. Stewards.

TCG is a multidisciplinary hospitality and tourism operator based in Playa del Carmen, Mexico. We develop and operate experience-sector businesses that perform independently and contribute collectively to the region's long-term economic stability.

The Origin

From one dive center

It started with XTC. In the early 2000s, a small team of divers began guiding people through the cenote systems of the Yucatan Peninsula. What started as a single operation in a remote coastal village became a question: what would it take to build this with institutional discipline?

The answer was TCG. A holding company designed to operate high-friction hospitality assets with financial precision, shared infrastructure, and a systematic approach to growth. Over 16 years and four active ventures, we have built and refined an operating model through direct experience in one of the most challenging tourism markets in the world.

Today, TCG operates four ventures, advises external clients, and serves as a proof-of-concept for what professionally managed, vertically integrated hospitality can look like in remote, high-barrier environments.

How We Build

Four pillars

Every TCG venture is built on the same structural foundation. These four principles are operating constraints, not aspirations.

01

Financial Durability

Each NUCLEO venture generates sufficient revenue to operate independently. Shared infrastructure is a multiplier, not a requirement. The portfolio is designed to remain viable even if individual sites operate in isolation, which means each business must justify its own existence before it benefits from the network.

Economics
02

Operational Integration

Shared logistics, staffing, back-office, and technology reduce the fixed cost base across all four sites. What would require four separate administrative teams operates under one centralized infrastructure layer, making every venture structurally leaner than a standalone operation of the same size.

Structure
03

Community Alignment

The communities that have managed these environments for generations are the primary beneficiaries of any development we build. Local employment, local sourcing, and co-development are built into the operating model. This is a structural commitment, not a positioning statement. Long-term viability in these markets requires community trust.

Impact
04

Ecological Discipline

TCG operates in some of the most sensitive marine and jungle environments in Mexico. Low-impact systems, certified standards, and documented operational commitments are prerequisites, not differentiators. The ecological health of our locations is the underlying asset of every venture we operate. Degrading it degrades the business. The two are the same thing.

Responsibility

Founder's Perspective

"Complexity is not something to avoid. It's something to design into."

Francisco Javier Salas Ahumada / Founder

Over two decades of building and operating in remote, regulated, and sensitive environments have shown us that profitability and sound environmental practice are codependent systems, not opposing forces. Each business we build becomes a platform for the next one, a foundation for local economic participation, and a test of whether high-quality operations can be sustained outside of resort corridors.

The Yucatan has taught us that business, when properly structured, can give back to the location it operates in as it scales. That is the operating thesis behind everything TCG builds.
